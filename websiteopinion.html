Website Opinion Articles

Great Website 1: Patagonia

Patagonia's website offers a user-friendly layout with an aesthetically pleasing theme. Their use of cards
and hooking images draws the user in and allows for fast-paced website navigation. While the Patagonia site
can be a lot to take in at first glance, a handy search bar can be found in the navigation section of the site
that can be used for efficient and effective searches. Aside from the visual side of the website, Patagonia
implements the box model that is well-known to every website developer and uses it effectively. They incorporate
HTML5 elements with complex CSS to enhance the elements throughout their website. Most websites are lacking
in any of these categories discussed above, but when it comes to website devlopment, Patagonia nailed it!

Great Website 2: National Parks

It goes without saying that everyone loves a good National Park. Whether it's Zion, Yellowstone, Mesa Verde
or Glacier, National Parks offer a great way for people to enjoy the outdoors. Better yet, having a well-developed
website helps people find these amazing places from their very own home! The National Parks website instills
a simple design with many different list items and buttons to allow for quick and easy access to information.
Along with their HTML5 elements, much like Patagonia's website, the National Parks website enhances their elements
with complex CSS to make their website pop. One little aspect that sets this website apart is their links to
their mobile apps included at the bottom of the page. Along with their links to their socials, this allows for
even more users to access their data and information.

Terrible Website: Yale School of Art

What do you think of when you hear Yale? I can tell you what I think: I think of very smart people who produce
quality products and gifts to the world. Apparently the Yale School of Art missed out on this, and ultimately
created the worst website I have ever seen in my entire life. It goes without saying that their website uses
an awful theme, but above all the organization of the entire site allows for anything but efficient navigation.
Their are very few HTML5 elements used on this site and the CSS that goes along with it might as well be created
by a toddler. Aside from all of these negatives, I do enjoy their calendar implementation in the middle of the
page. Aside from that, their is nothing pleasing about this site.
